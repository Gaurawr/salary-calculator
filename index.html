What is a salary calculator?

A salary calculator helps you find out exactly how much money you'll receive in hand after all deductions — PF, professional tax, income tax, and other cuts from your gross salary.

How is in-hand salary calculated?

In-hand = Gross salary − PF (employee) − Professional tax − Income tax − Other deductions. Your gross includes basic salary, HRA, and special allowances.

New regime vs old regime — which to choose?

The new tax regime (default from FY 2023-24) offers lower slab rates but removes most exemptions. The old regime allows deductions like HRA exemption, 80C (₹1.5L), and 80D (health insurance). Use the comparison above to see which saves you more.

What is EPF / PF deduction?

EPF (Employee Provident Fund) is 12% of your basic salary deducted every month. Your employer also contributes 12%. While it reduces your take-home, it builds a retirement corpus earning ~8.15% interest — tax-free on maturity.

Frequently asked questions

Is this updated for FY 2025-26?
Yes. Tax slabs, standard deduction (₹75,000), and new regime rules are updated as per Union Budget 2025-26.
Why is my actual salary different from this estimate?
Actual in-hand may differ due to company-specific benefits — meal vouchers, LTA, gratuity, ESOP, or city-specific professional tax slabs.
What is professional tax?
A state-level tax on employment income, capped at ₹2,400/year. Maharashtra, Karnataka, and West Bengal levy it. Delhi and Haryana do not.
Does bonus/variable pay affect my monthly in-hand?
Variable pay is part of your annual CTC but is usually paid quarterly or annually — not monthly. This calculator separates it so you can see your consistent monthly take-home.
Can I use this for both salaried and self-employed income?
This calculator is designed for salaried individuals with CTC-based packages. For self-employed or freelance income, tax calculation differs significantly.
