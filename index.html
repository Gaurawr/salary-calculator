How is your in-hand salary calculated?

Your in-hand (take-home) salary is your CTC minus all deductions: EPF/PF contribution, professional tax, income tax, and any other deductions from your employer. Here's the formula:

In-hand = Gross salary − PF − Professional tax − Income tax − Other deductions

Your gross salary includes basic salary, HRA, and special allowances. Bonus/variable pay is usually paid separately at year-end or quarterly.

What is basic salary?

Basic salary is typically 40–50% of your CTC. It is the foundation for PF calculation (12% of basic) and HRA calculation. Higher basic means higher PF but also higher taxable income.

New regime vs old regime — which should you choose?

The new tax regime (default from FY 2023-24) has lower slab rates but removes most exemptions like HRA, 80C, and 80D. It is better for people with low investments or high salaries. The old regime is better if you have significant deductions like home loan interest, 80C investments, and HRA exemption.

What is EPF / PF deduction?

Employee Provident Fund (EPF) is 12% of your basic salary, deducted every month. Your employer also contributes an equal 12%. While it reduces your take-home, it builds a retirement corpus and earns ~8.15% tax-free interest.

Frequently asked questions

Is this calculator updated for FY 2025-26?
Yes. Tax slabs, standard deduction (₹75,000), and new regime rules are updated as per Union Budget 2025-26.
Why is my actual in-hand different from this calculator?
This calculator gives a close estimate. Actual in-hand may differ due to company-specific benefits like meal vouchers, LTA, gratuity, ESOP, or city-specific professional tax slabs.
What is professional tax?
Professional tax is a state-level tax on employment income, capped at ₹2,400/year (₹200/month). Not all states levy it — Maharashtra, Karnataka, West Bengal do; Delhi and Haryana do not.
Does variable/bonus pay affect my monthly salary?
Variable pay is part of your annual CTC but is usually paid quarterly or annually, not every month. This calculator separates it from your fixed monthly salary so you can see your consistent take-home.
